Using ChatGPT Voice Mode in Higher Education

Center for Innovation in Teaching & Learning · University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

ChatGPT’s Voice Mode Voice Mode allows you to speak naturally with ChatGPT and hear real-time responses. offers faculty and staff a hands-free way to brainstorm, reflect, and prototype ideas. Whether you’re in the office, walking between classes, or facilitating a workshop, conversational AI can help you refine prompts, plan lessons, and think aloud in new ways.

Getting Started
  • Enable Voice Mode in the ChatGPT mobile app (tap the headset icon).
  • Grant microphone access Your microphone is only active during a session and can be turned off anytime. when prompted.
  • Select a preferred voice and start a natural conversation. Pause or switch topics anytime.
Practical Uses in Higher Ed
  • Faculty Development: Talk through teaching challenges or prompt design ideas aloud.
  • Course Planning: Rapidly brainstorm activity ideas or learning outcomes while on the go.
  • Reflective Teaching: Record thoughts on classroom dynamics or new tech integration for later notes.
  • Student Engagement: Model how to use generative AI responsibly and conversationally.
Tips for Teaching & Learning
  • Encourage students to use Voice Mode for language practice or reflective journaling.
  • Combine voice sessions with multimodal prompts Include images, screenshots, or charts for visual discussion. to enhance comprehension.
  • Use it to model metacognition: ask “How might I reframe this question?” aloud.
  • Remind users about privacy and data ethics Avoid sharing student names, grades, or any personally identifiable information. when using AI tools in teaching contexts.

Sample Faculty Development Chat

Instructor: I’ve been struggling to explain AI literacy to my students. How would you define it in one sentence?

ChatGPT (Voice): AI literacy is the ability to understand, evaluate, and ethically use artificial intelligence tools in personal and professional contexts.

Instructor: Nice. How might I make that engaging in class?

ChatGPT (Voice): Try starting with an exercise: ask students to describe the last time they relied on AI—knowingly or not. Then, discuss what “understanding” really meant in that case.

Instructor: Good. I could tie that to critical thinking and bias recognition.

ChatGPT (Voice): Exactly—framing AI as both a cognitive partner and a bias amplifier makes the discussion real and relevant.
