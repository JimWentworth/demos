University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign · Center for Innovation in Teaching & Learning

Feature comparison

Side‑by‑side overview of capabilities, privacy, security, and fit.
Feature Area Free / Plus (Individual) Teams / Enterprise (Business) Edu (Institutional Academic License)
Model Access Free: standard models with caps; Plus: access to advanced models with higher limits. Access to flagship models with high usage quotas for organizations. Access to flagship models; higher limits suitable for courses and research cohorts.
Tools & Capabilities Core chat and text generation; limited advanced tools. Full toolset: data analysis, web browsing, file uploads, coding support, long‑context. Full toolset oriented to teaching/learning: data analysis, document summarization, long uploads, browsing, coding.
Custom GPTs Personal use only; no organizational sharing. Create & share GPTs across a company workspace. Create & share GPTs across classes, departments, or campus workspaces.
Collaboration Single user. Shared workspaces, team folders, content governance. Campus‑wide deployment; supports groups for courses, labs, and research teams.
Data Use Policy Individual settings; some plans allow opting out of training. Business data is not used to train models. Student/faculty data is not used to train models; aligns with institutional privacy expectations.
Security & Compliance Consumer account security. Enterprise controls: SAML SSO, SCIM provisioning, audit, SOC 2. Enterprise‑grade controls plus education‑specific compliance considerations (e.g., FERPA awareness), SSO/SCIM, admin console.
Admin Controls None. Central admin, permissions, usage analytics. Central admin for IT/EdTech; usage analytics and retention controls.
Support Help center & community. Enterprise support & SLAs. Institutional onboarding and faculty development support pathways.
Cost Model Free or individual subscription. Per‑seat or org subscription (business). Institutional license (often discounted, scalable).
Best For Individuals, hobbyists, exploratory use. Businesses and professional teams. Universities, colleges, K‑12 districts, research institutes.

Key takeaways

Privacy & compliance matter most.Edu brings enterprise‑grade privacy and admin controls tailored for campuses.

It’s more than licensing.Edu typically includes higher usage limits, workspace sharing, and custom GPTs for courses/research.

Plan for pedagogy, not just access.Pair procurement with faculty development and clear syllabus policies for responsible use.
