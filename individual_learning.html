AI + Individualized Learning Pathways

Accessibility, Multimodal Design, and Support for Neurodiverse Learners

What You'll Explore

This interactive guide introduces how AI tools can support individualized learning pathways, enhance accessibility, and better serve neurodiverse learners. Use the accordions, tabs, and built-in voice features to explore key ideas.

Personalized Learning: chooses from a preset menu of options (e.g., select a topic, pick difficulty).

Individualized Pathways: dynamically generated sequences of supports, tasks, and modalities adapted to one learner’s needs.

Critical question: When might highly individualized pathways actually confuse or overwhelm learners instead of helping them?

  • Transform content across modes: text → audio → visuals → step-by-step breakdowns
  • Adjust pacing, chunk size, and complexity based on learner responses
  • Generate examples that connect to different backgrounds or interests
  • Support executive function by sequencing tasks and tracking progress
  • Reduces planning and organization load (executive function support)
  • Allows learners to select or shift modalities when sensory load is high
  • Adapts to daily variability in focus, energy, and attention
  • Offers private, stigma-free scaffolding before or alongside instructor support

Benefits: more access points, dynamic adjustments, stronger learner autonomy, and better fit for diverse learners.

Challenges: over-scaffolding, inaccurate explanations, equity and bias concerns, and privacy risks around sensitive learner data.

Prompt: Which of these challenges would you monitor first if you were designing an AI-supported intervention?

Design Guidance for Your Final Project

Your AI-enhanced intervention should clearly address:

  • Learner profile: Who are you designing for? How might neurodiversity show up in this context?
  • Barrier: What makes learning hard in this situation (beyond “the content is challenging”)?
  • AI capability: How will AI create or support an individualized multimodal pathway?
  • Accessibility: How do you account for access, usability, and inclusive design?
  • Benefits + challenges: What are the likely gains, and what concerns should be monitored?
  • Iteration: How would you gather feedback and refine your design over time?
