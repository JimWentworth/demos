Moving from Input/Output to Inquiry

Why Inquiry Matters

Most people begin their AI journey by treating the model like a search box: ask question → receive answer. This mindset caps the quality of what AI can do. Generative AI becomes most powerful when treated not as an answer machine, but as an inquiry partner that helps you explore assumptions, surface alternatives, and map conceptual space.

Key Shift: Stop asking AI for “the answer.” Start asking it to help you think better.

What Inquiry-Based AI Use Looks Like

Instead of focusing on one-off responses, inquiry uses back-and-forth questioning, expanding, critiquing, and refining. This mirrors real research, design thinking, and academic exploration.

Example: Inquiry vs. Input/Output

Input/Output mindset:

What are ideas for improving student engagement?

Inquiry mindset:

Generate 10 different lenses for understanding student engagement.
For each lens, propose a strategy and identify what assumptions it relies on.  
Then challenge each assumption with alternatives.

Guiding Principles of Inquiry-Based AI Collaboration

1. Treat AI as a thinking partner, not a shortcut

Inquiry prompts ask the model to slow down, question its own output, surface alternatives, or reveal what is missing. You’re shaping a conversation, not farming out the work.

2. Ask for structure, reasoning, and alternatives

Inquiry grows through contrast. Multiple interpretations reveal gaps and help you avoid premature certainty. 

Give me three different interpretations of this problem.
Identify what each interpretation highlights and what it obscures.
3. Use AI to map conceptual territory before narrowing

AI excels at breadth. Inquiry uses that strength deliberately before moving to judgment and selection.

Prompt Templates for Inquiry

Expand the Space 
Generate multiple framings of this issue.
Identify surprising angles that most people overlook.
Map the conceptual space and identify areas that seem underexplored.
Interrogate Assumptions 
What assumptions am I making?
Which of these are unnecessary, outdated, or untested?
Offer counterexamples that break these assumptions.
Challenge and Critique 
Critique the reasoning you just provided.
Identify weaknesses, oversights, or areas of overconfidence.
Suggest how the argument could be strengthened or reframed.
Refine and Synthesize 
Synthesize the strongest insights from the exploration so far.
Propose 2–3 coherent paths forward.
Highlight which path seems most promising and why.
