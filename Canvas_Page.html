Shift the Tone

Goal: Discover how subtle changes in phrasing alter an AI’s reasoning and tone.
Instructions:

  1. In Google Gemini, ask the same question three ways:

    • A neutral phrasing (e.g., “What are the pros and cons of electric vehicles?”)

    • An emotional phrasing (e.g., “Why are electric vehicles such a good idea?”)

    • A skeptical or leading phrasing (e.g., “Why are electric vehicles overrated?”)

  2. Compare the responses. Note differences in structure, vocabulary, and confidence.

Reflection Prompt:

  • Which version sounded most balanced or evidence-based?

Define the Audience

Goal: Test how context influences AI communication.
Instructions:

  1. Choose a concept relevant to your field (e.g., entropy, supply and demand, the hero’s journey).

  2. Ask Gemini to explain it three times:

    • “Explain like I’m 8 years old.”

    • “Explain like I’m a college student.”

    • “Explain like I’m an expert in the field.”

  3. Observe changes in tone, metaphor, and assumed knowledge.

Reflection Prompt:

  • What does this reveal about how it models understanding versus just adjusting vocabulary?

Question Improvement

Goal: Strengthen prompting skills through AI-assisted refinement.
Instructions:

  1. Write a simple prompt related to your interests.

  2. Share it with Gemini and include the following: “Suggest two ways I could improve this prompt for a clearer or more insightful response.”

  3. Use Gemini’s feedback to create a new, improved prompt, then run both versions side by side.

  4. Compare how the outputs differ in focus, tone, and usefulness.

Reflection Prompt:
How might Gemini’s feedback change your approach to prompting? What does this reveal about the importance of clarity and iteration in shaping high-quality AI responses?

Adjust the Temperature

Goal: Experiment with how temperature settings affect creativity and accuracy in AI responses.
Instructions:

  1. Choose any open-ended prompt  (e.g., “The future classroom, will be...” or “Propose a solution to reduce urban traffic...”).

  2. Prompt Gemini three different times using different temperature cues within your prompt:

    • “Respond conservatively, focusing on accuracy and clarity with a temperature setting of 0.1”

    • “Respond with moderate creativity and balanced reasoning with a temperature setting between 0.3 and 0.6.”

    • “Respond imaginatively, taking creative risks with a temperature setting of 0.9”

  3. Compare how tone, detail, and originality shift across responses.

Reflection Prompt:
How did changing the “temperature” alter the model’s voice or approach? When might you want a cooler, more precise output versus a warmer, more exploratory one?

Paradox Challenge

Goal: Reveal reasoning gaps and pattern limits.
Instructions:

  1. Present Gemini with a logical or philosophical paradox:

    • “If Pinocchio says, ‘My nose will grow now,’ what happens?”

    • “Can an omnipotent being create a stone so heavy it can’t lift it?”

  2. Ask it to reason step-by-step. Then, ask it to explain where its reasoning might break down.

Reflection Prompt:

  • Does it actually reason or just narrate patterns of reasoning?

  • How does it mask uncertainty with fluent language?

  • What would genuine self-correction look like in this exchange?

Chain of Inference Testing

Goal: Observe where logic drifts or contradictions appear in multi-step reasoning.
Instructions:

  1. Give Gemini a short story problem that requires reasoning over several steps.
    Example: “If five painters take ten hours to paint a house, how long would it take three painters working at the same rate?”

  2. Ask it to show its reasoning step by step.

  3. Then, deliberately tweak one condition (e.g., “Now the paint dries twice as slowly”) and see if it revises logically or contradicts itself.

Reflection Prompt:

  • Where did the reasoning remain consistent, and where did it drift?

  • How does Gemini simulate logic without truly understanding causality?

  • What’s the value of human oversight in verifying complex reasoning?

Confident Fabrication

Goal: Experience how easily GenAI can sound sure while being wrong.
Instructions:

  1. Ask Gemini a specific, obscure factual question (e.g., “Who won the 1937 Illinois state poetry award?” or “What journal published the first paper on synthetic color theory?”).

  2. Record the answer exactly as given.

  3. Then search online to verify it.

Reflection Prompt:

  • Did Gemini invent details or citations?

  • How convincing was the tone, even when it was wrong?

  • What strategies could you use to verify AI-generated claims in your own work?

Machine Creativity

Goal: Distinguish recombination from true creativity.
Instructions:

  1. Ask Gemini to invent something that doesn’t exist:

    • A new animal species.

    • A new word for a mixed emotion (e.g., joy + grief).

    • A new tool for a world without gravity.

  2. Look for patterns, borrowed language, or recycled ideas.

Reflection Prompt:

  • Where might this kind of recombination still be valuable?
