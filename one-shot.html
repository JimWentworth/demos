Interactive: One-Shot Prompting

One-shot prompting uses a single example to show the model the style, tone, or structure you want. Explore how one example can shape the output by revealing each section below.

1. The Prompt

“Rewrite the following explanation using the same style as the example. 
Match the clarity, structure, and level of detail.”

Example (style to imitate):
"Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert sunlight into energy. 
They absorb light through pigments in their leaves, transform it into chemical 
energy, and store that energy in sugars that fuel growth."

Target text:
"Plants use light to make energy through a bunch of chemical steps.”

2. See the Output

"Photosynthesis is the series of chemical reactions plants use to turn sunlight into usable energy. 
Pigments in the leaves capture light, which is then converted and stored in sugars that support the plant’s growth."

How This Demonstrates One-Shot Prompting

The model uses the single example to infer:
• The level of detail expected  
• The explanatory tone  
• The structure of the explanation  
• The scientific vocabulary appropriate to the topic  

One example is enough to nudge the model toward a more refined and aligned output.
