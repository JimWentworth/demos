Module 2 · Collaborative Iteration

Using Copilot for Multi-Stakeholder Reasoning

Use the campus version of Microsoft Copilot to explore a decision from multiple perspectives — then consider what YOU think.

Overview

In this activity, you will:

  • Describe your initial hypothesis or preferred solution for a campus-related decision.
  • Use the campus-licensed version of Microsoft Copilot to explore different stakeholder perspectives.
  • Refine your idea based on what you learn.
  • Reflect on how Copilot supported your thinking — and where your own judgment led.

Important: Do not include confidential, sensitive, or personally identifiable information in Copilot prompts. Use generic, fictional, or anonymized details.

Before you start, open the campus-licensed version of Microsoft Copilot in a separate browser tab so you can copy and paste prompts as you go: Open Copilot

How this connects to assessment

In the graded activity for this module, you will be asked to repeat this process with a new scenario and share your Copilot conversation fr review.   

Choose a realistic campus-related decision that matters in your context. This does not need to be a major initiative — choose any decision that involves multiple stakeholders and some tradeoffs.

If you are primarily a student, you might choose:
  • How to engage with student representation (e.g., student government, advisory boards) to initiate campus improvements.
  • How to connect with peers outside of the classroom to build community or mutual academic support.
  • How to use Copilot in your own learning while maintaining academic integrity.
If you are primarily staff or faculty, you might choose:
  • How to redesign a recurring meeting to make it more efficient and inclusive.
  • How to introduce Copilot into a workflow (e.g., email drafts, documentation, advising prep).
  • How to clarify expectations for student use of AI tools in a course or program.

  1. Check that the prompt already includes your decision text from Step 1.
  2. Click “Copy prompt” to copy it to your clipboard.
  3. Paste the prompt into Copilot and review what it surfaces about benefits, risks, and tradeoffs for that group.

Aim for at least three different stakeholders. You can jot insights separately (for example, in a document or directly in your quiz answers). If you closed it, you can reopen Copilot in a new tab.

Now update your idea in light of what Copilot surfaced — without treating its suggestions as automatically correct.

Before revising, quickly summarize for yourself:

  • Which stakeholder perspectives changed your thinking the most?
  • What new risks or tradeoffs are now on your radar?
  • Where are the biggest tensions between groups?

When you revise, you might:

  • Adjust the design of your idea.
  • Add safeguards, supports, or pilots.
  • Clarify what evidence you would want before committing.
Next, copy your refined version and paste it into Copilot. Ask: “What might still be missing from this revision, and what data or human expertise should I gather next?” You can open Copilot here: Open Copilot.

This is where you separate expanded perspective from final judgment.

For your own notes or a graded prompt (e.g., quiz or discussion), respond to at least two of these:

  • Where did you accept Copilot’s suggestions, and why?
  • Where did you deliberately override or ignore Copilot’s suggestions?
  • Which stakeholder perspective felt most underrepresented in your original idea?
  • What additional human expertise or real-world data would you want before implementing your decision?
  • How did this process change — if at all — your comfort with using Copilot in campus decision-making?
