AI-Assisted Course Design Resource Library

This library provides practical templates and prompts to help you leverage AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude for course development. Use these resources to streamline routine planning tasks so you can focus more energy on what matters most: your students, your research, and your teaching excellence.

What You Can Do With These Resources

  • Design complete courses with structured weekly plans and aligned assessments
  • Create engaging activities for any topic with step-by-step instructions
  • Generate discussion questions that promote critical thinking at multiple levels
  • Develop assignments with rubrics that clearly communicate expectations
  • Improve grading workflows with rubrics, comment banks, and efficiency strategies
  • Diagnose learning challenges and get targeted intervention strategies
  • Audit courses for inclusivity and accessibility
  • Convert courses to online or hybrid formats

Template 7: Discussion Question Generator ‚ñº

Use when: Creating discussion questions that drive deep thinking

Output: Layered questions from warm-up to challenging

Copy-Paste Template

I need high-quality discussion questions for my course.

TOPIC: [Specific topic, reading, or concept]
COURSE LEVEL: [Intro/Intermediate/Advanced]
DISCUSSION FORMAT: [In-class / Small groups / Online forum]
TIME AVAILABLE: [MINUTES or DAYS]

READING/CONTENT STUDENTS WILL HAVE:
[What they've read, watched, or experienced]

CREATE:
1. QUESTION LADDER:
   - Warm-up questions (accessible, everyone can answer)
   - Core questions (analysis, synthesis, evaluation)
   - Extension questions (challenge advanced thinkers)

2. FOR EACH CORE QUESTION:
   - Why this matters (learning purpose)
   - Expected responses
   - Follow-up probes if discussion stalls
   - Misconceptions this might reveal

3. FACILITATION TIPS:
   - How to launch discussion
   - Manage if no one responds
   - Ensure quieter students participate

AVOID yes/no questions and obvious answers. Focus on genuine thinking.

- Discussion facilitation tips

Best Practices for Using AI in Teaching

‚úÖ DO:

  • Use AI as a teaching assistant that amplifies your expertise
  • Provide specific context about your students, class size, and constraints
  • Iterate and refine responses with follow-up questions
  • Customize all suggestions for your unique situation
  • Verify factual content in your discipline
  • Apply your pedagogical judgment to all suggestions
  • Save time on routine tasks to focus on high-value teaching

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Never share student names or grades with external AI tools
  • Don't use AI responses without review and customization
  • Don't let AI make pedagogical decisions for you
  • Don't trust factual claims without verification
  • Don't skip customization - generic doesn't work
  • Don't use AI for high-stakes student decisions

‚ö° Pro Tips:

  • Context Front-Load: Provide your standard context once at conversation start
  • Iterate: Don't settle for first response - ask follow-ups
  • Be Specific: Include class size, student level, time, constraints
  • Use Negative Examples: Tell AI what NOT to include
  • Batch Similar Tasks: Handle related tasks together
  • Build Your Library: Save prompts that work well for you

Remember:

AI is your assistant, not your replacement. You bring irreplaceable expertise about your discipline, your students, and effective pedagogy. AI brings efficiency and fresh ideas. Together = better teaching with less burnout!
