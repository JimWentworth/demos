Exploring how artificial intelligence is reshaping teaching, learning, and institutions

AI offers the potential to tailor instruction, support individual learners at scale, and free up educators to focus on higher-order learning and human connection.

2. Critical Thinking & Ethical Use

It’s not enough to use AI — we have to ensure students are learning *how* to use it, when to rely on it, and how to stay aware of its limitations.

“At a metacognitive level, students … need to understand when they can use AI to just get a task done, and when and how they should use it for long-term learning.”
— Morgan Kelly, Dartmouth College (2025) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
“When educators talk about artificial intelligence … that excitement must be matched with caution, context, and critical awareness.”
— Stephanie Smith Budhai & Marie Heath (October 2025) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

3. Institutional Change & Policy

AI isn’t just a tool to plug into classrooms — it changes how institutions operate, how educators teach, and how we frame policy around learning at scale.

“AI is what I have called an ‘arrival technology.’ AI is not invited into schools through a process of adoption … it crashes the party and then starts rearranging the furniture.”
